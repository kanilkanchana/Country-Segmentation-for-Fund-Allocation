Project Overview

Business Problem

HELP International, an NGO, has raised $10 million. The CEO needs to decide how to allocate this money strategically to countries that are in the direst need of aid.

The Goal

Categorize countries using socio-economic and health factors to pinpoint those with the lowest overall development, forming a data-driven recommendation for fund allocation.

Dataset Details

The dataset comprises 167 countries with metrics including Child Mortality, Exports, Health spending, Imports, Income, Inflation, Life Expectancy, Fertility Rate, and GDPP.

Dimensionality Reduction (PCA)

To optimize the K-Means algorithm and reduce noise, we applied Principal Component Analysis (PCA), condensing 9 dimensions into 2 core components while retaining 62.92% of the total variance.

Strategic Insights & Recommendations

Key Findings

  • Economic-Health Link: Countries with Income < $4000 invariably suffer from Child Mortality rates > 90 per 1000 births.
  • Inflation Crisis: Critical risk areas face high inflation, rendering internal economic recovery near impossible without external aid.
  • Geographic Concentration: The overwhelming majority of the top priority nations are located in Sub-Saharan Africa.

Fund Allocation Strategy

Tier 1 ($7 Million Allocation)

Direct injection into Top 5 nations (Haiti, Sierra Leone, Central African Republic, Chad, Mali). Focus: Emergency medical supplies to combat child mortality and basic infrastructure.

Tier 2 ($3 Million Allocation)

Distributed among ranks 6-15. Focus: Long-term health sector development and import subsidies to combat inflation impacts.

Conclusion

By utilizing PCA and K-Means clustering, we successfully removed human bias from the selection process. The data unequivocally identifies a specific subset of nations trapped in a cycle of low GDP, hyperinflation, and devastating child mortality. Allocating the $10M fund to the highlighted Tier 1 and Tier 2 countries guarantees the highest humanitarian ROI.
